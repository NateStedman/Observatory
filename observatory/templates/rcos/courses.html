Course Offering

RPI offers an open source coding class separate from the general RCOS program, Open Source Software Practices ECSE 4968 / CSCI-4967.

To quote the syllabus:

Students who have successfully completed this course will be able to:
  • Demonstrate understanding of US copyright, patent and trademark laws and apply these concepts to the interpretation of software licenses.
  • Demonstrate understanding of the economics of peer-production and its business models, and recognize the conditions under which peer-production can be performed effectively.
  • Participate in open source projects, their governance and organizational structure.
  • Participate effectively in large scale software projects applying software engineering practices.
  • Recognize social implications of open source methodologies, such as open access publishing and the Wikipedia.
  • Understand the psychological and sociological basis of self-motivated work and how to apply them to foster emergent behaviors.
